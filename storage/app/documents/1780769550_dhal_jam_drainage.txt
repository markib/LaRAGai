DOCUMENT_ID: NP-PLUMB-002
TITLE: Troubleshooting Sewer Line Clogs (Dhal Jam) and Drainage in Nepal
CATEGORY: Wastewater & Drainage

Context:
In urban Nepalese constructions, drainage systems frequently choke (commonly known locally as "Dhal Jam") due to structural design flaws, narrow 3-inch pipes used where 4-inch pipes are standard, and high accumulation of household grease mixed with fine dust/silt from the local environment.

Standard Diagnostics for Dhal Jam:
1. Locate the Main Inspection Chamber (Sewer Safety Pit): Typically positioned in the compound or alleyway. If the chamber is dry but indoor toilets are backed up, the blockage lies within the internal building stack. If the chamber is overflowing, the blockage is between the property and the public municipal main line.
2. Mechanical Clearance: Do not rely heavily on imported chemical drain acids; cheap local pipe materials (thin-wall PVC) can warp or melt under exothermic reactions. Use a heavy-duty flexible steel drain snake wire or a high-pressure hydro-jetting machine.

Preventative Modifications:
- Install heavy-duty multi-layer grease traps (Charpi/Bhansako Channu) under kitchen commercial and residential sinks to isolate cooking oils before they hit the cold main drainage lines, where they solidify.
- Upgrade local bathroom floor traps to modern "P-trap" or "Bottle Trap" designs containing deep water seals to prevent foul sewer gases and insects from rising into living spaces.
